Meta's Wang Claims "Watermelon" Has Caught GPT-5.5 — In the Same Town Hall Where Zuckerberg Admitted Agents Have Stalled

At a July 2 internal town hall, Meta's chief AI officer Alexandr Wang told staff that Watermelon — the company's next frontier model, still training on roughly 10x the compute of April's Muse Spark — has "caught up" to OpenAI's GPT-5.5 on internal benchmarks. Meta hasn't named the tests, published numbers, or set a release date, so outside evaluators have nothing to check the claim against.

The more revealing line from the same meeting came from Mark Zuckerberg, who told employees Meta's AI agent work "hadn't accelerated in the way we expected" over the prior four months. Read together, it's a company simultaneously pitching a bigger, unverified future model to keep investor confidence up while admitting the near-term agentic product — the part actually shipping to users — is behind plan. That gap between the compute-scaling story and the product-delivery story is the real news, not the Watermelon claim itself.

Meta's Brain2Qwerty Turns Non-Invasive Brain Scans Into Typed Text

Meta AI's FAIR team published Brain2Qwerty, which decodes typing intent from non-invasive EEG/MEG brain recordings and reconstructs the typed sentence with no surgical implant required. It's markedly slower and less accurate than surgical brain-computer interfaces, but the no-surgery angle is what's driving the Hacker News debate — a workable non-invasive BCI would be usable at consumer scale in a way implant-based systems never will be.

Godot Game Engine Bans AI-Generated Code Contributions

The open-source Godot project will no longer accept pull requests containing AI-authored code, citing unresolved copyright provenance and a drop in patch quality from low-effort AI submissions. It's a small move next to today's bigger stories, but it's part of a widening pattern of open-source maintainers drawing a hard line on AI contributions — a policy question most major projects (the Linux kernel, Python) haven't formally settled yet.
