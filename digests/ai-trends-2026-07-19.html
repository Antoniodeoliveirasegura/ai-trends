Lead Story

China Launches a Rival Global AI Body, WAICO — 29 Countries In, US, UK, and Most of Europe Out

At the World AI Conference in Shanghai, Xi Jinping unveiled the World AI Cooperation Organization (WAICO), a new Shanghai-headquartered intergovernmental body signed by 29 founding members — including Russia, Belarus, Brazil, Cuba, and a bloc of African and Asian states — built to set global AI governance norms outside the US-led Hiroshima Process. Not one G7 country joined.

It matters because the world now has two competing AI governance blocs instead of one: Washington/Brussels/G7 on one side, Beijing and 28 mostly Global South states on the other. Analysts at the Simon Institute for Longterm Governance say the membership list looks deliberately built to give China outsized influence over AI standards across countries the West's frameworks don't reach — landing the same week open-weight Chinese models (Kimi K3, soon DeepSeek V4) are already out-benchmarking parts of the US frontier stack, giving WAICO's pitch of "AI sovereignty without Western gatekeeping" real technical teeth.
