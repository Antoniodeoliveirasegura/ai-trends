Google Gemini 3.5 Live Translate Rolls Out to Google Meet Enterprise — Near Real-Time Voice in 70+ Languages, System-Level, No Cloud Hop Visible to the User

Announced at WWDC on June 9 alongside iOS 27 and now in active enterprise rollout, Gemini 3.5 Live Translate does speech-to-speech translation across 70+ languages with low enough latency to feel synchronous. It runs at the OS layer on Pixel 10 and Galaxy S26 — voice calls translate in real time without routing through a user-visible cloud service. Baseten's June 22 round — led by Altimeter, Conviction, and Spark Capital — is one of the largest infrastructure raises in US startup history. The company runs 1 billion inference calls per day across 87 clusters on 18 cloud providers, with revenue up 20x year-over-year and valuation tripling in five months. Baseten is betting that the value in AI infrastructure has migrated from who builds the biggest model to who serves it fastest at lowest cost per token — and this round is the clearest capital-market affirmation of that thesis yet. Most companies will never train a frontier model; they will all need to run one.

DeepSeek Closes $7.4B — Only the Chinese State Gets Equity and Voting Rights; Commercial Investors Take a Five-Year LP Lockup with No Shares

DeepSeek has closed its first-ever funding round at more than 50 billion yuan ($7.4B), valuing the company at $52–59B. The deal structure is unprecedented in AI: founder Liang Wenfeng contributed 20B yuan himself; Tencent and CATL are the largest external investors. But commercial investors do not receive direct equity — capital flows into a limited partnership managed by Liang, subject to a five-year lock-up, with no voting rights and no direct corporate ownership. The National Artificial Intelligence Industry Investment Fund, a state-backed entity, received direct corporate ownership, voting rights, and exemption from the lock-up. China's government has effectively acquired a governance seat at the most capable AI lab to emerge outside the US — while letting private capital shoulder the financial risk. The Information called the structure a "founder-ownership masterclass"; analysts watching geopolitics call it structural state capture.

Quiet but Worth Watching

The Race for Orbital AI Data Centers Is Quietly Becoming Real — SpaceX, Axiom Space, and Starcloud Are Putting Compute Into Low-Earth Orbit

Axiom Space launched the first two dedicated orbital data center nodes to LEO in January 2026. SpaceX's S-1 (May 20) listed space-based compute as a core product line; its AI1 satellite — a 70-meter wingspan, 150 kW orbital compute node — is the first element of a planned constellation. Starcloud has already trained a language model in orbit using NVIDIA hardware and is scaling toward an 88,000-satellite network. The case for orbital inference: unlimited solar energy, no land or water footprint, potential sub-millisecond latencies for certain global routes. The case against: inference costs roughly $590 per billion tokens in orbit vs. $135 on Earth, radiation degrades hardware, and heat dissipation requires large radiative coolers. The cost gap will narrow as launch prices fall — and SpaceX's $60B acquisition of Cursor makes the "rocket to model" full-stack ambition more literal than anyone imagined a year ago. Not a 2026 story at scale, but the infrastructure is being laid now.
