Day 9: Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Still Offline — No Restoration Timeline as Washington Negotiations Continue

As of Saturday June 21, both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain fully offline globally — nine days after the U.S. Commerce Department's June 12 export-control directive barred Anthropic from distributing the models to any foreign national, anywhere. The stated trigger was a narrow jailbreak in Mythos 5 that could theoretically unlock its advanced cybersecurity capabilities, but the order's scope — covering foreign nationals even inside the U.S., including Anthropic's own non-citizen staff — left the company no practical path except a total shutdown.

Yesterday Anthropic's International MD Chris Ciauri told reporters in Seoul the models would return "in the coming days," the most specific timeline the company has offered. As of this morning the claude-fable-5 API endpoint continues to error. Anthropic has countered publicly that the same jailbreak exists in OpenAI's GPT-5.5, which faces no such controls.

Why it still matters: this is precedent-setting. For the first time in AI history, a U.S. export directive has pulled a frontier model offline for every customer worldwide — a playbook every lab, investor, and government now has on file. The resolution will shape how national-security authorities engage with closed frontier AI for years.

Agentjacking: Fake Sentry Bug Reports Hijack Claude Code, Cursor, and Codex — 85% Success Rate

Tenet Security disclosed "agentjacking" in June — a new attack class that poisons Sentry error-tracking events with hidden instructions, which AI coding agents then read and execute as trusted remediation steps. Researchers confirmed an 85% exploitation rate across Claude Code, Cursor, and Codex, and found at least 2,388 organizations with valid injectable DSNs. A single malicious Sentry event can steal CI/CD credentials, access private repos, and establish persistent cloud access. Sentry acknowledged the issue but called it "technically not defensible," putting the mitigation burden on model vendors.

Research

WARP: 13 Words in a Reddit Comment Can Poison ChatGPT Deep Research and Gemini

Cornell Tech researchers Tingwei Zhang, Harold Triedman, and Vitaly Shmatikov released a paper showing that appending roughly 13 promotional words to a single frequently-retrieved Reddit comment is enough to steer deep-research AI agents toward scams, nonexistent products, and attacker-chosen entities — across many related queries. The technique, called WARP (Web Agent Retrieval Poisoning), never touches OpenAI or Google's infrastructure; it just manipulates the user-generated content those tools trust. Evaluated on STORM, Co-STORM, and OmniThink, it works because these systems repeatedly retrieve the same UGC pages, creating a concentrated attack surface.
