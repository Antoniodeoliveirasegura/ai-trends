Anthropic's Fable 5 & Mythos 5 Remain Dark — Day Six of US Government Suspension

As of today, Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are still offline worldwide, following a US export-control directive issued on June 12 that cited a government-identified jailbreak technique capable of extracting known software vulnerabilities. Because Anthropic cannot filter foreign nationals from US users in real time, the company pulled both models entirely — affecting hundreds of millions of users — rather than risk violating the order.

Anthropic has publicly pushed back, arguing that the jailbreak is narrow, already present in competing models, and that applying this standard industry-wide would effectively freeze all new frontier deployments. Prediction markets (Kalshi) currently assign ~57% probability to restoration before July, with no official date given. The standoff is the sharpest collision yet between government AI-export policy and commercial AI rollout.

OpenAI's "Deployment Simulation" Paper — A New Safety Method That Flew Under the Radar

Published June 16–17, OpenAI's Deployment Simulation paper describes replaying 1.3 million de-identified real user conversations through a candidate model before release — stripping the original response and regenerating it — then scoring the new completions for behavioral drift, novel failure modes, and jailbreak susceptibility. Applied across GPT-5 Thinking through GPT-5.4, it caught misalignment patterns that static evals missed and was extended to agentic coding runs with tool calls. Overshadowed by the Fable 5 drama, this is arguably the most practically important alignment paper of the week: it closes the gap between lab evals and real-world behavior prediction, and sets a template other labs should copy.
