Quiet but Worth Watching

A New Benchmark Tests Whether AI Can Tell a Real Hazard From a Misleading Sign

EgoSafetyBench, a fresh arXiv benchmark, evaluates vision-language models as real-time safety guards for robots and wearables across 1,200 egocentric video scenarios — and finds that misleading in-scene text (a mislabeled sign, a false safety sticker) reliably fools even the strongest guards, which either miss genuine hazards or over-intervene on safe scenes. Quiet for now, but it's exactly the failure mode that starts to matter once VLM "safety guards" are supervising physical spaces instead of chat logs.
