Samsung Health Tells Users: Consent to AI Training, or We Delete Your Data

A new Samsung Health privacy prompt asks users to approve having their records, medication logs, sleep, activity, and cycle-tracking data used for AI training — and warns that declining disables account syncing and permanently deletes everything already synced. The story broke across 9to5Google, Cybernews, Android Police, Android Authority, SamMobile, and GSMArena on July 13-14, and shot to #1 on Hacker News; an early Android Authority poll found 86% of respondents refused to opt in.

It matters because it's a concrete, high-stakes test of "AI training consent" friction landing on hundreds of millions of real consumer devices holding genuinely sensitive health data. Privacy and legal commentators are already flagging it as a poor fit for GDPR-style "freely given consent" standards, since core functionality — and the survival of a user's own data — is conditioned on agreeing.
