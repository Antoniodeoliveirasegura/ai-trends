Lead Story

Fable 5 Returns Worldwide After US Lifts Its 19-Day Export Control — With a New Safety Classifier and a Credits-Gated Rollout

On June 30, the US Department of Commerce lifted its June 12 export control directive on Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5. The controls had been triggered after Amazon researchers discovered a method to prompt Fable 5 into identifying software vulnerabilities and, in one case, producing working exploit code — the government ordered both models suspended for all foreign nationals, including Anthropic employees, within hours. Reinstatement came after Anthropic agreed to proactively detect and address security risks, work with the government on future-release protocols, and deploy a new safety classifier that blocks the specific jailbreak technique in more than 99% of attempts.

Fable 5 went live again on July 1 across Claude.ai, the API, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork — but not unconditionally. Pro, Max, Team, and select Enterprise plans get it for up to 50% of weekly usage limits through July 7, after which it moves to a usage-credits model, a structural change from the flat-subscription access subscribers had before. The episode now stands as the first completed test of the US government's ability to impose and then negotiate real-time restrictions on a frontier AI model — and it establishes that such restrictions are both technically feasible and commercially consequential.

Models & Releases

Claude Sonnet 5 Ships June 30 as the Default Model for All Free and Pro Users — Near-Opus Performance at $2 per Million Tokens

Anthropic launched Claude Sonnet 5 on June 30 and made it the default for every user tier starting July 1. The model carries a 1-million-token context window, 128k max output tokens, and runs on a new tokenizer that generates ~30% more tokens for the same text. Performance benchmarks show it approaching Opus 4.8 on agentic workloads — planning, multi-step tool use, browser and terminal control — at introductory pricing of $2/MTok input and $10/MTok output through August 31. Early adopter feedback highlights that it "finishes tasks" where Sonnet 4.6 would stall, making it the first intermediate Anthropic model genuinely capable of unsupervised agent runs. Available via API, AWS Bedrock, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Foundry (preview).

Tooling

Z.ai Enters AI Coding With ZCode; Cursor Restructures Pricing July 1; GitHub Copilot Falls to 51% Market Share

Three moves are reshaping the ~$10B AI coding tools market this week. First, Chinese AI company Z.ai launched ZCode, a direct competitor to Cursor, Claude Code, and GitHub Copilot, targeting enterprise software teams. Second, Cursor split its Teams tier into two usage pools and added a Premium seat option, effective July 1 for new customers — a shift toward usage-based pricing that mirrors the broader industry move away from flat subscriptions. Third, the Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2026 showed GitHub Copilot's registered-user share falling from 67% to 51% year-over-year, attributed to prolonged outages and reported quality decline since late 2025 — even as Microsoft counts 26 million registered users and 90% Fortune 100 penetration. The combined picture: the market remains fast-growing but increasingly fragmented, and per-seat pricing is under structural pressure.

Research

UN Issues First Independent Global AI Assessment — Bengio and Ressa Chair Panel Warning Governance Cannot Keep Pace

The UN Independent International Scientific Panel on Artificial Intelligence released its preliminary report on July 1 — the first shared evidence base on AI prepared for all member states, co-chaired by Yoshua Bengio and Maria Ressa. Key findings: more than 1 billion people now use conversational AI every week; the United States controls 75% of the compute behind the world's top 500 AI supercomputers; and most countries — including many advanced economies — lack the technical expertise to independently evaluate frontier models. The panel's sharpest warning concerns agents: the length of software tasks that leading systems can complete has been doubling every 4–7 months, yet no scientific guarantees exist that agents will follow instructions reliably. Existing governance instruments are described as fragmented, concentrated among a handful of corporations, and rarely measured for real-world effectiveness. Findings are being presented at the UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance in Geneva, July 6–7.

Together AI Raises $800M Series C at $8.3B Valuation — Open-Source Inference Crosses $1B in Quarterly Bookings

Together AI, the San Francisco-based neocloud that rents GPU clusters optimized for open-source model inference, announced a $800M Series C on July 1, led by Aramco Ventures with participation from Vista Equity Partners, General Catalyst, Emergence Capital, Nvidia, and others. The round values the company at $8.3B — up from a $3.3B Series B just 16 months ago — and brings total funding to $1.3B. The headline metric: annual bookings surpassed $1.15B in Q2 2026. Together plans to scale its compute roughly 50-fold over five years. The raise is the clearest capital-market signal yet that open-source inference infrastructure is a durable business, not merely a cost center for companies that couldn't afford closed APIs.

Reflection AI's $6.3B SpaceX Compute Lease Begins — Two Ex-DeepMind Founders Paying $150M/Month for Colossus 2 GB300 Chips

Starting July 1, Reflection AI began paying SpaceX $150 million per month for access to Nvidia GB300 chips at the Colossus 2 data center in Memphis, Tennessee — a contract worth up to $6.3B if it runs through 2029. Reflection was founded in early 2024 by Misha Laskin (who led reward modeling for Gemini) and Ioannis Antonoglou (co-creator of AlphaGo), positioning itself as an open-source lab. SpaceX has now turned Colossus into a commercial AI compute platform, with Anthropic and Google paying $1.25B/month and $920M/month respectively. Context: at $150M/month, Reflection is the smallest Colossus tenant, but the deal's significance is structural — it signals that open-source labs are now competing for the same frontier-scale compute as closed incumbents, and winning access.

Culture & Discourse

Answer Engine Optimization Is Here: Companies Are Flooding Reddit With Fake Posts to Hijack What AI Tells You

A structural vulnerability in AI deep research tools — powering ChatGPT Deep Research, Gemini Deep Research, and major open-source systems — is being actively exploited. Researchers found that Reddit accounts for 54–71% of all user-generated content pulled by tested AI systems when constructing answers. Companies in peptide, HRT, and other consumer verticals have been seeding coordinated promotional posts designed to appear in AI-generated answers, a practice researchers are calling Answer Engine Optimization (AEO). The attacks are low-cost: security researchers documented chatbots repeating fabricated information within 24 hours of posting a fake article on a personal website. One incident involved DuckDuckGo's AI Search falsely reporting that Donald Trump and JD Vance died of rabies, traced to a coordinated Reddit seeding campaign. The episode has accelerated calls for AI providers to diversify their source weighting away from Reddit's concentration.

Quiet but Worth Watching

Five Eyes: "Frontier AI Will Transform Cyber Capabilities — The Timeline Is Months, Not Years"

On June 22, the intelligence alliance of the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand issued a joint statement asserting that frontier AI models are expected to "fundamentally transform both offensive and defensive cyber capabilities" — and that the timeline is "months, not years." The statement received far less attention than the Fable 5 saga unfolding simultaneously, but it is arguably the more durable signal: it's the first time all Five Eyes partners have jointly put a near-term timeframe on AI-driven cyber transformation in a public document. It suggests intelligence agencies are seeing capability curves in classified evaluations that haven't landed in public benchmarks yet — and it sets up a policy foundation for the kind of intervention the Fable 5 export control represented.
