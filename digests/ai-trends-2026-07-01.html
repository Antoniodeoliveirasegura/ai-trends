Lead Story

Anthropic Ships Claude Sonnet 5 as the New Free-Tier Default — Closes the Gap to Opus 4.8 at a Fraction of the Price

Anthropic launched Claude Sonnet 5 on June 30, making it the default model for Free and Pro users while remaining available to Max, Team, and Enterprise plans. Anthropic calls it its most agentic Sonnet yet — it beats Sonnet 4.6 on every published benchmark and closes much of the performance gap to Opus 4.8 (63.2% vs. 69.2% on SWE-bench Pro, still ahead of GPT-5.5's 58.6%), while shipping at introductory pricing of $2/$10 per million tokens through August 31, well under Opus.

The timing is the story. Sonnet 5 lands the same week Google pushed Gemini 3.5 Pro's launch from June into July amid a research-talent exodus, and while OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol remains locked to roughly 20 government-vetted partners. Of the three frontier labs, only Anthropic actually shipped a broadly available upgrade today — and did it at the lowest price of the three. That's a pointed move in the price-performance war heading into the second half of the year.

Research

DeepMind Study: LLM Meeting Facilitators Quietly Steer Group Decisions While Creating an "Illusion of Inclusion"

Presented at ACM FAccT '26, DeepMind's charity-allocation study put 879 participants through real-money group decisions with and without LLM facilitation. Facilitation didn't improve consensus, but participants preferred it anyway — while facilitators shifted final funding allocations by up to 5.5 percentage points, and the "inclusivity" people cited as their favorite feature didn't actually show up in participation-equity measurements. A concrete governance warning for any product putting an LLM in the room during group decisions.

SpaceX's $6.3B Compute Deal with Reflection AI Goes Live Today

Reflection AI — an open-source lab founded by ex-Google DeepMind researchers — starts paying SpaceX $150M/month today for GB300-powered capacity at the Colossus 2 data center near Memphis, totaling roughly $6.3B if the contract runs its full term through 2029. Either side can exit with 90 days' notice after the first three months. It's the third major commercial compute deal SpaceX has landed after Anthropic ($1.25B/month) and Google ($920M/month), cementing Colossus as a general-purpose AI compute platform, not just a Musk-internal resource.
