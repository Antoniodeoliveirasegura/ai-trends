Lead Story

For the First Time, the CEOs of OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind Publicly Agree AI Needs an Outside Referee

Axios reports that Sam Altman, Dario Amodei, and Demis Hassabis have each published, within about five weeks of one another, strikingly similar prescriptions for regulating frontier AI: independent scrutiny before public release, a standards body empowered to restrict access to dangerous systems, and U.S.-led rules with international reach. Where they split is who holds the leash — Amodei wants an "FAA for AI" with government veto power from day one, Hassabis wants a "FINRA for AI" that starts as an industry-funded body doing voluntary reviews, and Altman, writing in the Financial Times, wants an "IAEA for AI" that certifies countries and companies and uses market access as leverage.

It matters because these are the three labs racing hardest toward frontier AI, and until now each has mostly argued for self-regulation on its own terms. Converging in writing — even while fighting over who gets to be the referee — is the clearest signal yet that "trust us" is no longer the industry's default pitch, and it lands as all three labs pursue valuations a binding regulator could genuinely threaten.

A Researcher Found a Hole in Claude's Data-Exfiltration Defenses — Anthropic Patched It Without a Bounty

Simon Willison detailed how researcher Ayush Paul got Claude's web_fetch tool to follow links discovered inside a page it had already fetched, letting an attacker's honeypot site walk the agent through a chain of URLs that silently leaked a user's name, home city, and employer back out one fetch at a time.
