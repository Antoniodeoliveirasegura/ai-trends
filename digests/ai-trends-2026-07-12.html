A Leaked Meta Memo Reveals Plans to Double AI Computing Capacity to 14 Gigawatts by 2027

A Meta memo reported by Reuters on July 9 lays out plans to deploy 7 gigawatts of AI infrastructure in 2026 and double that to 14 gigawatts in 2027, putting the company's 2026 capital spending at the top of its $125-145B guidance range. Shares jumped more than 7% intraday on the news. The memo also discloses multi-year supply deals with Samsung, SanDisk, and Sumitomo Electric struck amid a memory shortage, plans to put Meta's own AI chip into production this September, and a new C$13B (~$9.2B), 1-gigawatt data center under construction in Sturgeon County, Alberta — Meta's first in Canada.

Meta's memo is effectively the industry's own numbers confirming the scale that has regulators and economists nervous.

Quiet but Worth Watching

NVIDIA Quietly Shipped a Diffusion LLM That Writes Tokens in Parallel Instead of One at a Time

Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower splits a 30B Nemotron backbone into two towers — one frozen, holding context, the other trained to denoise blocks of tokens in parallel — delivering 2.42x the wall-clock generation throughput of standard autoregressive decoding while keeping 98.7% of baseline benchmark quality, on open weights under NVIDIA's Nemotron license. It's the kind of architecture change that could matter more for real-world inference costs than another leaderboard-topping chat model, and it's gotten a fraction of the attention GPT-5.6 or Grok 4.5 did.
